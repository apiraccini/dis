# Data Privacy Policy

GDPR compliance and data handling procedures at Acme Corp.

## Data Classification

| Level | Definition | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Public | No harm if disclosed | Marketing materials, blog posts |
| Internal | Internal use only | Engineering docs, internal dashboards |
| Confidential | Limited access | Customer contracts, financial data |
| Restricted | Strictly controlled | PII, credentials, health data |

## Personally Identifiable Information (PII)

PII includes: name, email, phone, address, IP address, device IDs, payment info, and any data that can identify a natural person.

### Handling Rules

- **Storage** — PII must be encrypted at rest (AES-256). Use our key management service, not application-level encryption.
- **Retention** — Customer PII is retained for the duration of the contract plus 90 days. Delete after that.
- **Access** — Only employees with a direct business need may access PII. Access is logged and audited quarterly.
- **Transfer** — PII may not be transferred outside the EU/EEA without a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) in place.

## Data Breach Procedure

1. **Detect** — Any suspected breach must be reported to security@acme.internal immediately.
2. **Contain** — Isolate affected systems. Do not delete logs.
3. **Assess** — Security team determines scope within 4 hours.
4. **Notify** — If personal data is involved, notify the DPA within 72 hours (GDPR requirement). Notify affected users within 48 hours of confirmation.
5. **Remediate** — Fix the root cause. Post-mortem within 1 week.

## User Rights (GDPR)

Users may request:

- **Access** — What data we hold about them.
- **Rectification** — Correct inaccurate data.
- **Erasure** — Delete their data ("right to be forgotten").
- **Portability** — Export their data in machine-readable format.

All requests must be fulfilled within 30 days. Route requests to privacy@acme.internal.
